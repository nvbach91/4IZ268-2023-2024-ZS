Slavia History

Early days


Slavia Prague, one of the most iconic football clubs in the Czech Republic, traces its roots back to 1892 when it was founded by enthusiasts of the emerging sport. Initially established as the "Sportovní klub Slavia," the club quickly became a focal point for football development in the region. Its founding members, including Václav König and František Helfert, laid the foundation for a team that would go on to shape the history of Czech football.

The oldest photo of Slavia from 1898.


The Golden Era


Fast forward to the 1930s, and Slavia Prague was entering its golden era. The club achieved unprecedented success both domestically and on the European stage. In 1932, Slavia Prague reached the semi-finals of the European Cup, a remarkable feat at the time, though unfortunately being disqualified due to complications caused by the opposing team's (Juventus) fans. The team's impressive run showcased its ability to compete with the best in Europe and solidified its reputation as a football powerhouse.

Slavia's European comeback


In 1996, Slavia Prague etched its name in football history with a miraculous comeback in the UEFA Cup. Facing a 3-0 deficit against Roma, Slavia Prague stunned the football world by scoring an away goal towards the very end of the game, making the score 3-1 and advancing due to the previous 2-0 victory. The team went on to face Bordeaux in the semifinals, against whom they've lost both matches 1-0.

Today


As we step into the present, Slavia Prague continues to make waves in European football. Recent years have seen the club reclaim its position among the continent's elite, with notable performances in the UEFA Europa League. The team's tactical prowess, coupled with the development of young talent, has garnered attention and admiration. Off the pitch, Slavia Prague remains deeply connected to its community, with a commitment to nurturing local talent and fostering a strong sense of identity. The club's recent successes signal a promising chapter in its storied history, leaving fans eager to witness the next glorious moments that Slavia Prague is bound to create.
