All about Blender

What is Blender?

Blender is a free and open-source 3D creation suite that supports every aspect of the 3D pipeline, from modeling, rigging, animation, and simulation to rendering, compositing, and motion graphics. It is a powerful tool that can be used to create everything from animated films and visual effects to 3D-printed models and interactive applications.

Blender is a cross-platform application that runs on Windows, macOS, and Linux. It is also available in a variety of languages.

Blender is a popular choice for both professional and hobbyist 3D artists. It is a powerful and versatile tool that can be used to create a wide variety of 3D content.

What is Blender Fundation?

The Blender Foundation (2002) is an independent public benefit organization with the purpose to provide a complete, free and open source 3D creation pipeline, managed by public projects on blender.org. Its spin-off corporation Blender Institute (2007) hosts the foundation’s offices and currently employs 24 people who work on the Blender software and creative projects to validate and stress Blender in production environments.

The Foundation’s income is via donations and the Development Fund. The Institute revenues come from Blender Studio, the Blender Store and sponsoring. This enables Ton Roosendaal and a small staff to work full-time on Blender, organize activities for SIGGRAPH or the Blender Conference, support development, documentation and training projects, maintain and host blender.org services, and pay for bookkeeping and administration expenses.

What is Blender?

Blender Institute formally acts as the working company of Blender Foundation. To keep the Foundation lean and a safe place for Blender, almost all activities of the Foundation are being conducted by the Blender Institute. That includes office rent, daily administration, general management, HR services, Blender Store, Blender Conference organization, event participation (SIGGRAPH, Annecy, etc), operational support for blender.org, managing Development Fund grants, software research projects, workshops, meetings, and so on.
