Blender Featured in Prestigious Animation Film Festival

Published on September 21, 2023

The Blender community is celebrating the success of several Blender-created short films that have been selected for screening at the prestigious Annecy International Animation Film Festival. The festival, held annually in Annecy, France, is one of the most renowned events in the animation industry, showcasing the best and brightest works from around the world.

Among the Blender-created films selected for this year's festival are:

  • "The Prospector" by Jonas Åkerlund, a captivating tale of a lone prospector seeking gold in a desolate landscape.
  • "A Place to Call Home" by Wannes Lefeber, a heartwarming story about a young boy finding solace in an abandoned house.
  • "The Golden Ticket" by Nicolas Weideman, a whimsical adventure following a group of children on a magical train ride.

These short films, all created using the Blender 3D creation suite, demonstrate the remarkable artistic achievements that can be realized with open-source software. The Blender community is proud to see its work featured among the world's top animation studios at this prestigious event.

Blender 3.6 LTS Released: A Stable Foundation for Creative Projects

Published on October 4, 2023

The Blender Foundation has proudly announced the release of Blender 3.6 LTS, the latest long-term support (LTS) version of the popular open-source 3D creation suite. Blender 3.6 LTS is a culmination of over a year of development, bringing a host of stability and performance enhancements, along with several new features to empower artists and designers alike.
