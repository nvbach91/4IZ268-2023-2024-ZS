GP Brazil Results

The BWT Alpine F1 Team secured a commendable seven points in today's São Paulo Grand Prix, showcasing a resilient performance at Interlagos. Pierre Gasly exhibited remarkable skill, navigating from fifteenth on the starting grid to an impressive seventh-place finish at the checkered flag. Simultaneously, Esteban Ocon executed a strong Sunday effort, progressing from fourteenth on the grid to secure the tenth position.

The race dynamics took a dramatic turn with a multi-car incident on the opening lap, prompting a red flag. Both Esteban and Pierre demonstrated adept reactions, skillfully navigating through the chaos to gain several positions. Upon the restart, Pierre continued his ascent, gaining two more places to secure the ninth position, while Esteban maintained a solid performance, advancing to tenth.

During the 71-lap race, strategic decisions played a pivotal role for our team. Esteban executed a three-stop strategy (Soft-Medium-Soft-Soft), while Pierre followed a two-stop plan (Soft-Medium-Soft), with both drivers concluding the race on fresh Soft tires. On lap 49, Pierre capitalized on a tyre advantage over Esteban, who allowed him to pass in pursuit of Lewis Hamilton. Demonstrating impressive pace and securing the Fastest Lap of the race at the time, Pierre skillfully closed the gap on the seven-time world champion. A well-executed maneuver into Turn 1 elevated Pierre to eighth place, ultimately securing seventh position after all cars completed their pit stops.

Esteban made his final pit stop a few laps later, with the objective of narrowing the gap to George Russell's Mercedes for the last available point. Russell retired on lap 59, facilitating Esteban's smooth journey to a tenth-place finish.

Pierre’s Comment

“I’m very happy with today’s race and I would say it was one of our best performances of the season. It was a very challenging race to manage with multiple stints and pit-stops, but the entire team did a great job today to make the right calls and execute the stops well. For the team, in seventh and tenth, it’s clearly a strong performance, especially after starting from near the back of the grid. We competed with Carlos [Sainz] and the two Mercedes throughout, so that is clearly a good step forwards from yesterday. Next up is Las Vegas, which will no doubt be a cool experience. I’m excited for it, as I always am for new races, and it is up to us to enter that race as prepared as possible and give it our all on-track.”

Esteban’s Comment

“Today we had a small reward with one point after a challenging race. We had a great start, gaining some positions while also avoiding the chaos in front. Unfortunately, we saw very quickly that we had very high degradation throughout and had to go for a three-stop race. We had planned for this as we saw some signs yesterday that this could be a factor, so that is something we will assess going forward. Nevertheless, a good day in the office for the team with both cars in the points and a good step since yesterday’s performance in the Sprint Race. Our focus now shifts to Las Vegas, the penultimate race of the season, which promises to be a spectacular experience where we hope to deliver another strong result.”
