Our History

Summary

The team boasts a rich heritage, debuting in Formula One in 1981 under the name Toleman, headquartered in Witney, England. In 1986, following its acquisition by Benetton Group, the team underwent a name change to Benetton and clinched the 1995 Constructors' Championship. By the 2000 season, Renault had acquired the team for the first time, leading to a name change to Renault F1 Team in 2002. The team secured Constructors' Championships in 2005 and 2006, with Fernando Alonso winning the Drivers' Championships in the same years. In September 2020, Renault Group communicated their plan to adopt the name "Alpine" for the factory team in order to promote the Alpine brand. Consequently, the team is poised to be recognized as the "Alpine F1 Team," marking the retirement of the "Renault F1 Team" name after a five-year tenure.

Toleman Motorsport

Toleman marked the inception of Alpine in Formula One in 1981, participating in 70 Grands Prix without securing a championship. Despite limited success, the team holds historical significance for launching Ayrton Senna's F1 career in 1984, where he secured three podium finishes, including a memorable second place in Monaco. In May 1985, Toleman rebranded as Benetton Formula after securing major sponsorship from Benetton.

Benetton

Benetton's journey began with the B186 powered by a BMW engine in 1986, designed by Rory Byrne. A pivotal shift occurred in 1987 when the team signed a long-term deal with Ford, welcoming notable drivers. Under Flavio Briatore's leadership, featuring three-time World Champion Nelson Piquet, Benetton finished third in the Constructors' Championship in 1990. The arrival of Michael Schumacher in 1991 brought hope for a championship, realized in 1994 amid intense battles and controversies, including Ayrton Senna's tragic death. In 1995, Schumacher secured another title for Benetton with a Renault engine, and Johnny Herbert's contributions led to the team's first and only Constructors' Championship. The subsequent years brought challenges, with Ferrari's interest, key personnel departures, and Renault taking over the team in 2000, fully rebranding it as the Renault F1 Team in 2002.

Renault F1 Team

Renault, evolving from the Toleman and Benetton legacy, embarked on a quest for championships. Jenson Button started the journey in 2003, giving way to Fernando Alonso in 2004. Against Ferrari and McLaren dominance, Renault secured back-to-back championships in 2005 and 2006. Fernando Alonso, in 2005, became the youngest-ever F1 World Champion, claiming 15 podiums and seven wins. In 2006, Alonso and Renault triumphed again in Brazil, clinching another title. Despite challenges, including race-fixing allegations in 2009, Renault underwent changes, with most of the team acquired by Genii Capital, leading to the arrival of Lotus in 2011.

Lotus Renault GP

Lotus Renault GP, born from Lotus Group's investment in Renault, clashed with Team Lotus led by Tony Fernandes. Legal battles ensued, exacerbated by both teams utilizing Renault power units. Complications arose, with Robert Kubica's near-fatal crash prompting his withdrawal. In the following year, Lotus transitioned to Lotus F1 Team, while Fernandes acquired Caterham. Struggling with Renault engines, Lotus turned to Mercedes for a new power unit in 2015. Meanwhile, Renault, not fully ready for the hybrid era, branched out to create a new Formula One team.

Renault Sport Formula One Team

In 2015, Renault revealed plans to take over Lotus F1 Team, transitioning to Renault Sport Formula One Team in 2016. Red Bull Racing became an engine customer until 2018, with controversies surrounding the Renault engine's performance. Red Bull switched to Honda in 2019, and Renault faced challenges, losing McLaren as a customer to Mercedes. Despite limited success, Daniel Ricciardo secured a podium at the 2020 Eifel Grand Prix. In September 2020, Renault announced a rebrand, racing as Alpine F1 Team in 2021.

Alpine

In 2021, Alpine, formerly Renault, made a significant comeback in F1, rebranding and bringing back star driver Fernando Alonso. Despite ups and downs, the French team competes well in the midfield, with Alonso and teammate Esteban Ocon challenging top drivers. Alpine achieved a historic win at the 2021 Hungarian Grand Prix, securing their first-ever victory with Ocon. This marked the first win for a French driver in a French car since Alain Prost in 1983. Alonso also shone on the podium, finishing third in the inaugural Qatar Grand Prix in 2021.
