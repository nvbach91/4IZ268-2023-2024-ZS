History

The team boasts a rich heritage, debuting in Formula One in 1981 under the name Toleman, headquartered in Witney, England. In 1986, following its acquisition by Benetton Group, the team underwent a name change to Benetton and clinched the 1995 Constructors' Championship. By the 2000 season, Renault had acquired the team for the first time, leading to a name change to Renault F1 Team in 2002. The team secured Constructors' Championships in 2005 and 2006, with Fernando Alonso winning the Drivers' Championships in the same years. In September 2020, Renault Group communicated their plan to adopt the name "Alpine" for the factory team in order to promote the Alpine brand. Consequently, the team is poised to be recognized as the "Alpine F1 Team," marking the retirement of the "Renault F1 Team" name after a five-year tenure.
