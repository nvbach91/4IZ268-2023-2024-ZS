How to play

The beauty of the game is how simple you can play it. Just place different pieces on the board as they fall and try to fill the entire row without any blank spaces. If you manage to do so, the row will disappear, and you will gain points depending how many lines/rows you cleared at once. The most points can be scored by placing the long bar in the way that you clear all four lines which is maximum and you are rewarded with maximum points - that is called Tetris.

Even though how simple the gameplay is, there are many strategies and techniques of playing. Let's take a look on a few basic tips that can improve your high score. Below, there are three main playing techniques you can practice. Note: all of the information is dedicated to the classic version - NES Tetris. However, most of the tips can be used in other versions as well.

  1. Start with slower speed and try to be as consistent as you can.
  2. Keep your stack flat and build your extra pieces on the left because of rotation of the pieces.
  3. Learn all rotations of pieces.
  4. Go to higher speeds when you learned all above.
  5. Score as many tetrises as you can.
  6. Practice better technique.

Techniques

DAS

There are three main techniques how to play on your NES controller. First one is called DAS - delayed auto shift. It is important to perfect your timing when new pieces fall down. This method is the easiest but it is not sustainable for higher speeds (level 19+).

Hypertapping

Second technique is called hypertapping and was invented few years ago by new young players. The name explains itself. You need to press the controller very fast to be able to move pieces around. With mastering this you can achieve higher level speeds such as speed after level 29.

Rolling

However, almost 40 years after the game was created, new technique came - rolling. You roll all your fingers from the bottom of the controller to achieve more presses of the button than hypertappers.
