About the Toronto Maple Leafs

The Toronto Maple Leafs are a professional ice hockey team based in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. Founded in 1917, they are one of the oldest and most storied franchises in the NHL.

The team's iconic blue and white jerseys are recognized by hockey fans around the world. The Maple Leafs have a dedicated fanbase and are known for their passionate supporters.

The organization has a rich history filled with memorable moments, legendary players, and a commitment to excellence on and off the ice.

Team History

The Maple Leafs have a storied history in the NHL, with a total of 13 Stanley Cup championships. Over the years, the team has seen the likes of Johnny Bower, Darryl Sittler, and Mats Sundin, who have become iconic figures in the hockey world.

Despite challenges and periods of rebuilding, the Maple Leafs continue to strive for success. The team's commitment to developing young talent and fostering a winning culture ensures a promising future.

Fans have witnessed historic moments, intense rivalries, and the unwavering spirit of the Maple Leafs throughout their journey in the NHL.

Home Arena

The Toronto Maple Leafs play their home games at the Scotiabank Arena, located in the heart of downtown Toronto. The arena, with a seating capacity of over 19,000, provides a thrilling atmosphere for fans.

Scotiabank Arena has hosted numerous playoff battles, memorable victories, and championship celebrations. It stands as a symbol of the team's connection with its passionate fanbase.

Whether it's the roar of the crowd or the iconic chants, the home arena is an integral part of the Maple Leafs' identity.
