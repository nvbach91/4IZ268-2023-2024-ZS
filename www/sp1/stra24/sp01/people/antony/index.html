Marc Antony

Marcus Antonius, commonly known as Mark Antony, was a prominent Roman general and statesman whose intricate ties with Augustus, the first Roman Emperor, shaped the course of Roman history. Initially allied through the Second Triumvirate alongside Lepidus, their relationship soured over time, leading to the famed conflict between Antony and Augustus, culminating in the Battle of Actium in 31 BCE.

Despite their earlier collaboration, the enmity between Antony and Augustus played a pivotal role in the rise of Augustus and the eventual establishment of the Roman Empire.
