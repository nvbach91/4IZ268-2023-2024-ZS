Achievements
and important events

Battle of Actium (31 BCE)

Augustus, along with his naval commander Agrippa, defeated the forces of Mark Antony and Cleopatra at the Battle of Actium.

After the Battle of Actium, Cleopatra and Mark Antony committed suicide, solidifying Augustus's control over the Roman world.

Establishing the empire (27-23 BCE)

In 27 BCE, Augustus handed over power to the Roman Senate but retained significant authority.

Augustus received additional powers and titles in 23 BCE, consolidating his authority further. This included the titles "Augustus" and "Princeps," signifying his unique position as the first among equals. This marked the beginning of the Roman Empire, and Augustus became the first emperor.

Pax Romana (27 BCE - 180 CE)

Augustus initiated a long period of relative peace and stability known as the Pax Romana. This era lasted for approximately two centuries and was characterized by minimal military conflicts within the empire.

Building Projects (27 BCE - 14 CE)

Augustus initiated numerous building projects throughout the Roman Empire, including the construction of temples, roads, and aqueducts. The most famous of these is the Forum of Augustus and the Temple of Augustus in Rome.

Legislation and Reforms

Augustus implemented various legal and administrative reforms, aimed at restoring traditional Roman values and social order. His legislation included moral laws to promote family values and encourage childbirth.

Death of Augustus (14 CE)

Augustus died on August 19, 14 CE. His death marked the end of his 41-year reign and the beginning of the rule of his adopted son Tiberius, continuing the Julio-Claudian dynasty.
