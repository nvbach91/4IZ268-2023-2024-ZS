Warfare History Blog - Austerlitz

Units of the Austrian empire

Grenadiers - Austria

Originaly, grenadiers were explosive specialists who used hand grenades as offensive weapons during sieges in 17th century. While this practice was no longer used during Napoleonic wars, greadiers still retained the trait of being composed of the strongest and largest soldiers in the army. They were often used to lead assaults such as storming fortifications during sieges.

Line infantry - Austria

Most numerous and versatile portion of any army, which served as its backbone. At this time, an austrian line regiment typically consisted of 6 fusilier companies in each of the field batallion - Leib and Oberst. Fusilier here refers to normal musketman, since line regiment normally included specialized units, such as grenadiers. Each regiment had its own zone of recruitment within the multicultural empire.

Grenzers

These units were considered something between light and line infantry, however they were mostly used as skirmishers. Before the reforms of 1809, their inability to fight as well in line as standart line infantry regiments was seen as weakness by austrian leadership. However, they regularly performed very well in battle and had great deal of respect from Napoleon.

Units of the Russian Empire

Imperial guard - Russia

Throughout the Napoleonic Wars the Imperial Russian Guard was commanded by Grand Duke Konstantin. At Austerlitz in 1805 the infantry of the Guard included: Guard Infantry Division, commanded by General Lieutenant Pyotr Malutin, Guard Cavalry Division - General Lieutenant Andrei Kologrivov and artillery of the Guard.

Line infantry - Russia

Most numerous and versatile portion of any army, which served as its backbone. Administratively, the regiments were assigned to Military Inspections, the predecessors of military districts, and included the conscript training depots, garrisons and fortress troops and munitions magazines. The army had been thoroughly reorganised on the Prussian model by the emperor's father Paul I.

Cossacs - Russia

Cossacs were semi-regular units and in the Russian Empire and historically, they were a semi-nomadic and semi-militarized people. The Cossacks were organized into several voiskos (hosts), named after the regions of their location. While they were famous for being one of the best horsemen of the time, some infantry units of cossacs also existed.
