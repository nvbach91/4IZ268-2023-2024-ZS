My Crocheting story

I started crocheting when I was 17. At that time I became sick and could not visit school for 3 weeks, so I've found myself in crocheting. Slowly but surely my whole family got their new winter hats, and I've found a way to make my closest family members and friend happy.

Winter hats & scarves

I really wanted to have my own fluffy winter hat, so it was my first and my most loved project. I started by doing the simplest winter hats possible - soon after that all of my family members had their own hat. It was easy, fun, relaxing, and also very warm for a cold winter. After winter hats I immediately started with scarves - a great (in my opinion) addition to a cool hat.

Soon-to-be made projects

Nowadays I am dreaming of making small crochet toys, but unfortunately I did not have time to start. I believe that it will also be easy and fun, and I love giving gifts to my friends, so I will have more options to choose.

Tips and Tricks

Starting Your Crochet Journey

Begin with Simple Projects: Start with easy patterns like scarves or coasters. This builds your confidence and skills gradually.

Understanding Yarn: Research about different types of yarn and their suitability for various projects. I recommend medium weight yarn for the begginers.

Mastering the Basics

Be consistent: Maintaining a consistent yarn tension is key to uniform stitches. Practice makes perfect.

Reading Patterns and Charts: Take time to familiarize yourself with common crochet symbols and abbreviations.

Overcoming Common Challenges

Twisted Chains: When starting with a chain, ensure it's not twisted before joining it into a round. Using stitch markers can help.

Uneven Edges: If your edges are uneven, check if you’re missing stitches or adding extra ones. Counting stitches in each row can prevent this.

Additional Tips

Keep Your Hands Relaxed: Crocheting for long periods can strain your hands. Take breaks and stretch your fingers.
